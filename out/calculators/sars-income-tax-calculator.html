    How South African Income Tax Works (2026/2027)

    Progressive Tax Brackets

    South Africa uses a progressive tax system where higher earners pay a higher percentage of tax. For the 2026/2027 tax year, rates range from 18% on the first R245,100 up to 45% on income above R1,878,600.

    Tax Rebates

    All taxpayers receive a primary rebate of R 17 820,00. Taxpayers aged 65–74 receive an additional secondary rebate of R 9 765,00, and those 75 and older receive a further tertiary rebate of R 3 249,00.

    Medical Tax Credits

    If you contribute to a medical aid, you qualify for monthly tax credits: R 376,00 for yourself, R 376,00 for the first dependent, and R 254,00 for each additional dependent.

    Retirement Fund Deductions

    Contributions to approved retirement funds (pension, provident, or retirement annuity) are deductible up to 27.5% of your remuneration, with an annual cap of R 350 000,00.

    Tax Thresholds (2026/2027)

    You are not required to pay income tax if your annual taxable income is below these thresholds:

    • Under 65 yearsR 99 000,00
    • 65 to 74 yearsR 153 250,00
    • 75 years and olderR 171 300,00
